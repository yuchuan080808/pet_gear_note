    Best Enclosure Covers for Dogs

    Compare popular enclosure covers for dogs, with buyer cautions, best-fit scenarios, and practical tips before you buy.
    May 11, 2026 · Pet Gear Note

    Most dogs don’t want a room with a 360-degree view. A bare wire cage leaves them visually exposed to every passing cat, mail carrier, or flashing TV screen in your house.

    If your dog barks at every shadow or struggles to settle down, an enclosure cover is the fastest way to artificially create the dark, den-like environment they actually want. But slapping a bedsheet over a metal frame is a quick way to create a suffocation hazard or a shredded mess.

    Buying the wrong cover usually results in torn fabric, broken zippers, or restricted airflow that overheats your dog. You have to match the material to the dog’s behavior and the location of the crate. Indoor anxiety requires breathable blackout fabric, while outdoor runs need heavy-duty, weather-resistant tarps.

    If you have a serious escape artist or a dog with clinical separation anxiety, a piece of fabric won’t solve the root issue. You’re better off consulting your vet about Best Prescription Medications for Dogs to manage their panic alongside structural crate training.

    How We Read This List#

    We look straight past the manufacturer’s promises of “indestructible” materials. If a dog wants to pull fabric through the wire bars and chew it, they will.

    Instead, we scan marketplace signals for practical failures. We look for flimsy zippers, water pooling on outdoor roofs, and missing attachment ties. A good cover should fit snugly enough to avoid flapping in the wind, but hang loose enough to allow adequate ventilation.

    Buying Guide: Avoiding Enclosure Regrets#

    Does it block enough light for sleep?#

    Light synthetic fabrics might diffuse harsh sunlight, but they won’t completely black out a room. If you need total darkness to stop morning whining, look for covers with a specific blackout layer or thick 600D polyester. Just remember that thicker fabric traps more heat, making proper ventilation non-negotiable.

    Are fabric covers safe for power chewers?#

    Absolutely not. No soft fabric can withstand a bored, anxious dog with access to the edges. If your dog obsessively pulls blankets through the cage bars, do not buy a form-fitting cover. Instead, give them a high-value distraction like one of the Best Bully Sticks for Dogs to keep their mouth busy while they settle.

    How do you prevent overheating?#

    Airflow is your primary safety concern. Never zip a dog into a fully enclosed fabric box without open mesh panels or rolled-up window flaps. For outdoor kennels, ensure the cover sits high enough above the dog to allow cross-breeze, and always provide secondary shade. You can read more about safe outdoor temperature limits from the American Kennel Club.

    Final Summary#

    Choosing the right cover comes down to respecting the environment you are placing it in. If you are crate training indoors, a lightweight, highly ventilated mesh option like the Explore Land will keep a puppy calm without baking them. If you are managing outdoor working dogs, you absolutely need heavy-duty, multi-layered barriers like the PawGiant 8x4ft to combat UV rays. Stop expecting a thin sheet of fabric to survive a chewing dog, prioritize airflow above all else, and you’ll skip the buyer’s remorse entirely.
