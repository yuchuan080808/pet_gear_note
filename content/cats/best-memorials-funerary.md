## Buying Guide

### Securing Ashes, Fur, and Whiskers
The biggest risk with wearable or small containment memorials is accidental opening. **Never trust a simple friction-fit cork for permanent storage.** Vials designed with standard corks are notorious for working loose over time. If you use a glass bottle for fur or whiskers, you must seal the cork with clear adhesive. **For ashes, insist on threaded, screw-top closures**, like those found on stainless steel cremation jewelry, to guarantee the contents stay safe.

### Outdoor Durability and Weight
Garden memorials face rain, UV rays, and wind. **Resin and poly-resin statues are highly detailed but dangerously lightweight.** If placed in an open garden, high winds or wild animals can easily tip or relocate a hollow resin marker. If you want a zero-maintenance outdoor marker, **solid granite is vastly superior to resin or painted concrete**, as it will not chip, fade, or blow away in a storm. 

### Managing Customization Expectations
Laser-engraved photo stones are a popular choice, but buyers often misunderstand the final result. **Laser etching on black granite produces a grayscale, flat image**, not a high-definition color photograph. Images with low contrast or heavy shadows rarely translate well to stone. To avoid regret, **submit the brightest, highest-contrast photo you have**, and expect a stylized etching rather than a perfect photograph.

### Planning Paw Print Kits
Clay impression kits require timing. **These must be done while the cat is alive, or coordinated immediately with your vet after passing.** Many owners wait too long or find their older cats deeply uncooperative with having their paws pressed into clay. **The clay often dries thinner than expected**, meaning you must handle the finished discs carefully to avoid snapping them during display.
