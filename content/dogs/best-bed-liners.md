A high-quality orthopedic dog bed is an investment, but its foam core is essentially a giant sponge. Once urine, vomit, or wet-dog oils breach the outer fabric, that expensive mattress is often ruined permanently. 

Bed liners solve this by creating a barrier between the messy dog and the vulnerable foam. However, buying the wrong liner can create entirely new problems. Cheap plastics create a crinkling noise that terrifies anxious dogs, while poorly designed zippers trap air and turn the bed into a balloon. 

You need a liner that blocks moisture without sounding like a grocery bag. Your choice depends heavily on whether you are protecting a flat crate pad, a bulky orthopedic mattress, or your own human bed.

## Buying Guide

**The "Balloon" Effect**
A truly 100% waterproof liner with a tight zipper will trap air inside the bed. When your dog steps on it, the bed balloons up, which can scare timid dogs or burst the zipper. **Always leave the zipper open about one inch** to allow trapped air to escape when your dog lies down. 

**Noise and Texture Constraints**
Many budget liners use a stiff TPU backing that crinkles loudly. If your dog startles easily or circles extensively before lying down, avoid thin plastic liners. Look for liners with a soft fabric face or invest in high-quality [best bed covers for dogs](/dogs/best-bed-covers/) to muffle the sound.

**Chew and Dig Risks**
Liners are rarely built to withstand teeth or nails. They are designed to sit securely under a durable outer cover. If you use a thin zippered liner directly exposed to a dog that digs or chews, it will likely rip within a single day. 

**Washing Limitations**
Waterproof coatings break down under high heat. **Never put a waterproof liner in a hot dryer** or iron the waterproof layer. Machine wash on cold and hang dry to prevent the backing from peeling, cracking, or melting.
