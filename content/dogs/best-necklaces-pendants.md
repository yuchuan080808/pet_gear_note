## Buying Guide

### Are dog necklaces safe for unsupervised wear?
Never leave a decorative necklace, beaded chain, or dangling pendant on your dog when they are unattended. **Choking hazards are the primary risk** with pet jewelry. If a bead string snaps or a metal link catches on a crate wire, your dog cannot free themselves. Only use these items for supervised walks, photoshoots, or events. If your dog is crated, remove all accessories. For crate safety, stick to environment upgrades like the [Best Crate Covers for Dogs](/dogs/best-crate-covers/).

### Can I attach a leash to a dog necklace?
Absolutely not. Decorative chains, pearls, and beaded necklaces are built with weak clasps and thin strings. **They will snap under the tension of a pulling dog**, allowing your dog to escape. Always attach your leash to a properly fitted flat collar or harness. You can layer a decorative necklace over or under their walking collar for the desired look.

### How do I size jewelry for a fluffy dog?
If your dog has a thick double coat, hard metal chains or tight beads will disappear in their fur and cause uncomfortable matting. Opt for **slide-on accessories** that attach to their existing flat collar, or choose oversized items like the Nite Ize LED tube that sit loosely on top of the ruff. If you want to dress up a fluffy dog without a necklace, consider styling their topknot with the [Best Hair Accessories for Dogs](/dogs/best-hair-accessories/).

### What materials trigger canine skin allergies?
Cheap metal alloys and faux-gold plating can cause contact dermatitis on dogs with sensitive skin. If your dog loses hair around their neck or develops red rashes, remove metal jewelry immediately. Stick to fabric accessories or slide-on collar attachments that do not directly touch the skin. Alternatively, look for small accessories that attach to existing gear, such as the [Best Collar Charms for Dogs](/dogs/best-collar-charms/).
