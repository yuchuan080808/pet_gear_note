## Buying Guide

### Does "waterproof" really mean waterproof?
Most quilted dog covers are **water-resistant, not fully waterproof**. While an inner TPU layer slows down liquid, heavy accidents that sit for hours will often eventually seep through the stitching. If your dog frequently soils their resting area, you need a heavy-duty oxford liner or a specialized incontinence pad beneath the primary cover. 

### Is the cover safe for destructive chewers?
No fabric bed cover will survive a dedicated chewer. If your dog actively shreds and ingests bedding, remove soft covers entirely to prevent dangerous intestinal blockages. For dogs that require strict containment rather than plush bedding, consider durable [enclosure covers for dogs](/dogs/best-enclosure-covers/) or heavy-duty [crate covers for dogs](/dogs/best-crate-covers/) to create a secure, den-like environment.

### Will it stay put on leather furniture?
Silicone backing dots provide moderate grip on fabric couches, but **they frequently fail on smooth leather**. Active dogs jumping on and off the furniture will bunch up most blanket-style covers. If you have leather seats, expect to straighten the cover daily.

### What are the hidden snag hazards?
If your dog wears [necklaces & pendants for dogs](/dogs/best-necklaces-pendants/), watch out for exposed zippers or loose quilt stitching on bed covers that could snag their jewelry. Always choose covers with hidden zipper tracks or envelope closures for safety. 

### How should you adapt to the seasons?
Just as you match [hats for dogs](/dogs/best-hats/) to the weather, match your bed cover material to the season. Use **breathable mesh or smooth oxford cloth** in the summer, and switch to plush fleece in the winter. If your dog spends long afternoons on an outdoor cot wearing [sunglasses for dogs](/dogs/best-sunglasses/) for UV protection, a mesh replacement cover prevents dangerous overheating from below.
